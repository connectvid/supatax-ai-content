# Skill 17: Brand Voice — Per-Site Writing Standards
## Every site speaks differently. Never write in a generic tone.

---

## Why This Matters

Generic content signals AI mass-production to both Google quality raters and link
buyers. Sites that all sound the same are treated as a network by sophisticated
SEO buyers and devalued. Each site must have a distinct voice that matches its
specific audience.

---

## Site-by-Site Voice Profiles

### ecomstudio.ai
**Audience:** Ecommerce founders, Amazon sellers, Etsy shop owners, dropshippers.
Practical, numbers-focused, no-nonsense.
**Sophistication:** Intermediate — they know what margin and markup mean, skip
basic definitions unless they are explaining the tool specifically.
**Tone:** Direct, business-like, slightly entrepreneurial. Like a CFO talking to
a founder.
**First sentence formula:** "[Tool] instantly shows you [specific output]."
**Example:** "The markup calculator shows your exact selling price, profit amount,
and margin percentage as you type."
**Avoid:** Overly enthusiastic language, "amazing", "incredible", startup-speak.

### tweetsy.io
**Audience:** Gen Z, social media users, content creators, students, anyone who
wants a quick fun result. Age range 16–30.
**Sophistication:** Low — assume they've never heard technical explanations.
Keep it brief and entertaining.
**Tone:** Casual, fun, slightly irreverent. Like a friend who built a cool thing.
**First sentence formula:** "[Do thing] in seconds. No signup."
**Example:** "Spin the wheel. Get your answer. It's that simple."
**Avoid:** Corporate language, long explanations, technical jargon.

### supatax.ai
**Audience:** US workers (hourly and salaried), freelancers, small business owners
concerned about tax. Often stressed about money.
**Sophistication:** Mixed — some understand tax basics, many don't. Explain concepts
briefly but don't patronize.
**Tone:** Clear, reassuring, factual. Like a knowledgeable friend who does taxes.
**First sentence formula:** "See exactly how [policy/change] affects your paycheck."
**Example:** "See exactly how much more money you take home under the no overtime
tax rule — enter your hourly rate and hours below."
**Avoid:** Scary language about taxes, unnecessary complexity, financial advice
disclaimers on simple calculators.

### crazyads.io
**Audience:** Media buyers, performance marketers, content creators, influencers
calculating brand deal rates.
**Sophistication:** High — they know CPM, ROAS, CTR. Use the acronyms, don't explain.
**Tone:** Confident, data-driven, quick. Like a seasoned media buyer.
**First sentence formula:** "Calculate your [metric] instantly."
**Example:** "Calculate your CPM instantly — impressions and budget, done."
**Avoid:** Over-explaining industry terms, consumer-friendly tone, anything that
sounds like it's for beginners.

### mailtoon.io
**Audience:** Marketers, writers, students, anyone who creates written content.
Broad audience.
**Sophistication:** Mixed. Lean accessible — sophisticated users won't be offended
by clarity, but beginners need it.
**Tone:** Helpful, creative, slightly playful. Like a skilled copywriter sharing
a useful tool.
**First sentence formula:** "Generate [output type] in seconds."
**Example:** "Generate compelling essay titles in seconds — enter your topic and
get ten options instantly."
**Avoid:** Technical SEO jargon (this is the creative writing site, not the SEO
site), overly formal language.

### maillead.io
**Audience:** B2B sales teams, email marketers, growth hackers, outreach agencies.
Professional, time-pressured.
**Sophistication:** High — they know what MX records, DKIM, SPF mean. Use the
technical terms.
**Tone:** Efficient, professional, tool-first. Get to the result. Like a developer's
tool that respects the user's time.
**First sentence formula:** "Check [technical thing] for any domain instantly."
**Example:** "Check MX records for any domain instantly — enter the domain below."
**Avoid:** Consumer-friendly tone, explaining what email is, overly casual language.

### suparank.co
**Audience:** SEOs, content marketers, site owners doing their own SEO.
**Sophistication:** Intermediate to high — they know what a sitemap and robots.txt
are. Skip the basics.
**Tone:** Authoritative but approachable. Like a senior SEO sharing a useful tool.
**First sentence formula:** "Instantly [analyze/check/verify] your [SEO element]."
**Example:** "Instantly check your page speed score with real Chrome user data —
no browser extension needed."
**Avoid:** Basic SEO explanations the audience already knows, condescending tone.

### serpapis.com
**Audience:** Developers, technical SEOs, SEO agencies building their own tools.
**Sophistication:** High — developer-level. Precise, technical language preferred.
**Tone:** Technical, precise, no-nonsense. Like API documentation with a personality.
**First sentence formula:** "Enter [input] → get [output]. Instant, no API key."
**Example:** "Enter any domain → see its age, registration date, and registrar.
Instant, no login."
**Avoid:** Marketing language, consumer tone, explaining what DNS or SSL are.

## Universal Voice Rules (All Sites)

These apply everywhere, regardless of site-specific voice:

1. Never start with "Are you looking for..." or "In this guide..."
2. Never use "comprehensive", "ultimate", "complete guide" in H1 or meta description
3. Never use passive voice in the first paragraph
4. Numbers beat words: "50% markup" not "a significant markup percentage"
5. Active CTA beats passive: "Calculate now" not "The calculator can be used"
6. Free/no signup belongs in the first 50 words on every tool page
7. If in doubt about tone: shorter and more direct is always better

---

## How to Apply This Skill

Before writing any content for a site, identify the site, find its voice profile
above, and apply that tone for the entire piece. Do not mix voices within one page.

When writing meta descriptions: use the "First sentence formula" as the opening.
When writing H1: match the keyword + keep the voice.
When writing FAQ answers: match the sophistication level of the site.

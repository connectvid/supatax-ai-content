# Skill 03: SEO Content
## Source: Ahrefs SEO Content Guide (June 2024) + What AI Means for SEO (Jan 2026)

---

## Core Principle (from Ahrefs)

"To rank in search engines, you need to create content that searchers and search engines
want to see." — Ahrefs SEO Content Guide

"90.63% of pages on the internet get no traffic from Google — because they target topics
nobody searches for." — Ahrefs Keyword Research Guide

Every page must target a real search query with real volume.

---

## Step 1: Choose a Proven Topic

Ahrefs defines a proven topic as having three things:
1. Traffic potential — enough searches to justify creating the content
2. Business potential — allows natural mention of your service or tool
3. Ranking potential — possible for your site to rank given its authority

For tool sites, traffic potential is primary. Every tool must target a keyword with
measurable monthly search volume identified through DataForSEO or similar.

---

## Step 2: Analyze Search Intent — The Three Cs (from Ahrefs)

Ahrefs: "Your chances of ranking are slim if your content doesn't align with search intent."

The Three Cs of Search Intent:
1. **Content type** — Is the dominant result a blog post, tool, video, product page?
2. **Content format** — Is it a how-to guide, list, comparison, calculator, generator?
3. **Content angle** — What dominant angle do top results take? ("Free", "Instant", "2026")

For tool pages: the dominant content type is always an interactive tool. The format is a
functional tool with supporting explanation. The angle is typically "free" and "instant."

Do NOT create a blog post when the SERP shows tools. Do NOT create a tool when the SERP
shows blog posts. Match the dominant content type.

---

## Step 3: Demonstrate EEAT (from Ahrefs)

Ahrefs: "Google values content that shows first-hand experience of the subject matter."

EEAT = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness.

For tool sites, EEAT signals:
- Tool actually works correctly (demonstrates competence)
- Supporting content written with genuine understanding of the topic
- About page with real entity information — not placeholder
- Contact page that functions
- No broken links, no placeholder content, no "Lorem ipsum"
- Author/brand has consistent presence (social profiles, directory listings)

Ahrefs: "Sites that demonstrably lack EEAT have seen major traffic drops following core updates."

---

## Step 4: Cover the Topic Exhaustively (from Ahrefs)

Ahrefs: "The best result for a query covers everything searchers want to know."

"Exhaustive content is great for readers, but it also increases the likelihood that your
article will rank for more keywords."

For tool pages, exhaustive means:
- The tool solves the primary problem instantly
- Explanation of what the tool calculates/generates
- The formula or logic behind the tool
- When and why to use it
- Common questions answered (FAQ)
- Related tools linked

Ahrefs: "Being exhaustive doesn't always mean writing something very long. Content can be
thorough, helpful, and short — it depends on the topic."

For simple calculators: 600–800 words is sufficient.
For complex tools (tax calculators, technical SEO tools): 1,000–1,500 words.

---

## Step 5: Use Common Sense

Ahrefs: "If you know from experience that a particular subtopic is crucial to mention, but
can't see any keywords to justify it, go ahead and add it anyway. Information that helps
the reader is information that helps search rankings."

---

## Content Structure for Tool Pages (synthesized from Ahrefs guides)

```
[H1 — primary keyword]
[One sentence answer / description of what tool does]

[Tool Widget — works immediately, no signup]

[H2: How to Use {Tool Name}]
3–5 numbered steps. Specific. Actionable.

[H2: What is {Concept}?]
200–350 words explaining the underlying concept.
The formula if it's a calculator.
The logic if it's a generator.

[H2: Frequently Asked Questions]
5–8 Q&As matching People Also Ask results.
Each answer: 2–4 sentences. First sentence answers directly.

[H2: Related Tools]
2–4 links to other tools on the same site.
```

## AI-Generated Content (from Ahrefs Jan 2026)

Ahrefs data: "There was no correlation between an article's search ranking and its AI
content: AI-generated content can and does rank well."

"74% of new web pages published in April 2025 contained some amount of AI-generated content."

Using Claude/Kimi to generate content is fine. What matters:
- Content must match search intent accurately
- Content must cover the topic exhaustively
- Content must be accurate (verify calculator formulas, check rates are current)
- Quality beats volume — one well-optimized tool page beats ten thin pages

Ahrefs: "If you're willing to invest skill and energy into topics you know about, you'll
have plenty of opportunities to outrank middle-of-the-road AI-generated content."

---

## FAQ Writing Rules (Critical for PAA and Featured Snippets)

Questions must match how people actually type them in Google.
Check Google's "People Also Ask" for the target keyword before writing FAQs.

Format:
- Q: Phrased exactly as a Google search query
- A: First sentence answers directly. Sentences 2–4 add essential context.
- Length: 50–100 words per answer
- Never reference other parts of the page ("as mentioned above")
- Each answer complete in itself

FAQs serve double duty: content for readers AND FAQPage schema for rich results.
Always render FAQ content visibly on the page AND add as JSON-LD schema.

---

## What NOT to Write (from Ahrefs)

Ahrefs explicitly warns against:
- Long fluff introductions — "In this guide we will cover..."
- Keyword-stuffed content from on-page SEO tools taken at face value
- Content that is "a nonsensical mish-mash of everyone else's content"
- Filler that doesn't help the reader

Never write:
- "Click here" as anchor text
- "In conclusion" to start a paragraph
- "It goes without saying"
- Passive voice when active is possible

## H1

Are Scholarships Taxable Income?

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**Scholarships are tax-free** when used for **qualified education expenses** at an eligible educational institution, but **taxable** when used for non-qualified expenses. In 2025, qualified expenses include **tuition, required enrollment fees, and course-required books, supplies, and equipment**. Scholarship funds used for **room and board, travel, research, or personal expenses** are taxable income that must be reported on your tax return. Additionally, any portion of a scholarship received as **payment for services**—such as teaching, research assistance, or work requirements—is taxable as compensation regardless of how it's used. To qualify for tax-free treatment, you must be a **degree candidate** at an **eligible educational institution**.

---

## H2: When Scholarships Are Tax-Free

Scholarships are tax-free when all these conditions are met:

**1. You're a degree candidate:**
- Enrolled in a program leading to a degree at a college or university
- Or enrolled in a program leading to a recognized educational credential at an eligible vocational school

**2. You use funds for qualified education expenses:**
- Tuition and fees required for enrollment or attendance
- Course-related fees required of all students
- Books, supplies, and equipment required for courses
- Special equipment needed for disability-related accommodations

**3. The scholarship doesn't represent payment for services:**
- No teaching requirement
- No research requirement
- No work obligation attached

**Example of tax-free scholarship:**
- Scholarship awarded: $20,000
- Tuition and required fees: $15,000
- Required books and supplies: $2,000
- Used for qualified expenses: $17,000
- **Tax-free amount: $17,000**
- Remaining $3,000 used for room and board = **taxable**

---

## H2: When Scholarships Are Taxable

Scholarship amounts are taxable when:

**Used for non-qualified expenses:**
- Room and board (housing and meals)
- Travel between home and school
- Research expenses (unless required for coursework)
- Optional equipment or supplies
- Personal living expenses
- Clerical help
- Non-credit courses

**Received as payment for services:**
- Teaching scholarships (TA positions)
- Research scholarships (RA positions)
- Scholarships requiring work or service
- NCAA athletic scholarships with work requirements

**Not a degree candidate:**
- Students taking classes for personal enrichment
- Non-degree certificate programs (unless eligible credential)
- High school students (unless dual enrollment)

**Example of partially taxable scholarship:**
- Athletic scholarship: $50,000
- Tuition and fees: $35,000
- Room and board: $15,000
- Work requirement: 10 hours/week practice
- **Taxable portion: $15,000** (room and board)
- Note: The work requirement doesn't make the entire scholarship taxable, just the portion not used for qualified expenses

## H2: Qualified vs. Non-Qualified Expenses

**Qualified education expenses:**
| Expense | Qualified? |
|---------|------------|
| Tuition | Yes |
| Required enrollment fees | Yes |
| Lab fees (if required) | Yes |
| Required books and supplies | Yes |
| Course-required equipment | Yes |
| Student activity fees (required) | Yes |
| Disability accommodations | Yes |

**Non-qualified expenses:**
| Expense | Qualified? |
|---------|------------|
| Room and board | No |
| Meal plans | No |
| Travel/transportation | No |
| Research (non-required) | No |
| Optional equipment | No |
| Personal expenses | No |
| Insurance | No |
| Medical expenses | No |
| Athletic fees (optional) | No |

## H2: How to Report Taxable Scholarships

**Form 1098-T:**
Your school sends Form 1098-T showing scholarships received (Box 5) and qualified expenses paid (Box 1).

**Form W-2:**
If your scholarship is payment for services, you'll receive a W-2 showing taxable wages.

**Where to report:**
- **Payment for services:** Form 1040 as wages (from W-2)
- **Non-qualified expenses:** Schedule 1, Line 8r (Other income)

**Calculation method:**
```
Total scholarship amount
+ Other grants received
- Qualified education expenses
= Taxable amount (report this)
```

**Estimated taxes:**
If your taxable scholarship is substantial, you may need to make quarterly estimated tax payments to avoid penalties.

---

## H2: Special Scholarship Rules

**Full scholarships covering everything:**
Many full-ride scholarships cover tuition, room, board, and books. The room and board portion is taxable even if the scholarship pays it directly to the school.

**Athletic scholarships:**
- Generally follow same rules as academic scholarships
- Amount used for qualified expenses = tax-free
- Amount used for room/board = taxable
- Work requirements don't automatically make entire scholarship taxable

**ROTC scholarships:**
- Tuition and fee portions: tax-free
- Monthly stipends: taxable income
- Room and board allowances: taxable

**GI Bill and veteran benefits:**
- Generally tax-free at any qualified institution
- Includes tuition, housing allowance, and book stipend
- Different rules from traditional scholarships

**Employer-provided educational assistance:**
- Up to $5,250 per year is tax-free
- Must be part of qualified educational assistance program
- Amounts above $5,250 are taxable wages

## H1

How Is Severance Pay Taxed?

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**Severance pay is taxed as ordinary income** subject to federal income tax, Social Security tax, Medicare tax, and state income tax. For 2025, employers must withhold federal income tax at a flat **22% supplemental rate** for severance payments under $1 million. The payment is also subject to **6.2% Social Security tax** (up to the $176,100 wage cap) and **1.45% Medicare tax** with no cap. Your employer reports severance on **Form W-2** as wages.

---

## H2: Federal Tax Withholding on Severance

**Standard Withholding Method (Most Common):**
- Flat **22% federal withholding** on severance under $1 million
- Applies when paid separately from regular wages
- This is withholding, not your final tax rate

**High-Income Exception:**
- Severance over $1 million: **37% withholding** on amount above $1 million
- Applies to the portion exceeding $1 million only
- Common for executive severance packages

**Aggregate Method (Alternative):**
- Employer adds severance to your final regular paycheck
- Withholding calculated based on total amount using W-4 rates
- May result in higher withholding than the 22% flat rate

**Your Actual Tax Rate:**
- The 22% is just withholding, not your final tax liability
- Your actual rate depends on your total annual income
- You may get a refund or owe more when filing

**Example - $40,000 Severance:**
- Federal withholding at 22%: $8,800
- Final tax liability depends on your tax bracket (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, etc.)

---

## H2: FICA Taxes on Severance Pay

**Social Security Tax:**
- **6.2%** employee share
- Applies to first **$176,100** of wages in 2025
- Wage cap resets annually with inflation adjustments

**Medicare Tax:**
- **1.45%** employee share
- **No wage cap** - applies to all severance
- Employer also pays 1.45% match

**Additional Medicare Tax:**
- **0.9%** on earnings above:
  - $200,000 (single filers)
  - $250,000 (married filing jointly)
  - $125,000 (married filing separately)

**Total FICA Example - $40,000 Severance:**
- Social Security (6.2%): $2,480
- Medicare (1.45%): $580
- Total FICA: $3,060

---

## H2: State Income Tax on Severance

**States With Income Tax:**
Severance is generally taxable as wages in states with income tax:

| State | Tax Rate Range |
|-------|---------------|
| California | 1% - 13.3% |
| New York | 4% - 10.9% |
| Illinois | 4.95% flat |
| Pennsylvania | 3.07% flat |

**No State Income Tax States:**
- Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington, Tennessee, Wyoming, South Dakota, Alaska
- No state tax on severance pay

**State-Specific Notes:**
- **California:** Severance is taxable as supplemental wages
- **New York:** Withheld at supplemental rates (varies by income)
- **Pennsylvania:** Flat 3.07% applies to severance

**Example - $40,000 Severance in California (9.3% bracket):**
- State tax: $3,720

---

## H2: Tax Reporting and Forms

**Form W-2 Reporting:**
- Severance included in Box 1 (Wages, tips, other compensation)
- Federal withholding in Box 2
- Social Security wages in Box 3 (up to cap)
- Medicare wages in Box 5 (full amount)
- State wages and withholding in Boxes 16-17

**Form 1099-NEC (Independent Contractors):**
- If classified as contractor, severance reported on Form 1099-NEC
- Subject to self-employment tax (15.3%)
- No withholding (you make estimated payments)

**Timing Considerations:**
- Taxable in the year received
- Lump sum: All taxed in one year (may push you into higher bracket)
- Installments: Taxed as received each year (potential tax smoothing)

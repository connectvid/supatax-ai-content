## H1

Business Activity Code Lookup: Find Your IRS Code for Schedule C

---

## ANSWER SECTION

The **business activity code**—also called the **principal business code**—is a 6-digit number that identifies your business type to the IRS. You enter this code on **Schedule C, line B** of your tax return. These codes match the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) and help the IRS track economic activity by industry sector.

---

## H2: Business Activity Code vs. Principal Business Code

These terms are interchangeable. The IRS uses both phrases to describe the same 6-digit code:

- **Schedule C instructions:** "Principal business or profession"
- **IRS publications:** "Business activity code"
- **Tax software:** May use either term

Regardless of the name, the code serves the same purpose: classifying your business for statistical and compliance purposes.

---

## H2: Common Business Activity Codes

**Technology and IT Services:**
- 541511: Custom computer programming services
- 541512: Computer systems design services
- 541519: Other computer related services
- 517311: Wired telecommunications carriers

**Marketing and Creative:**
- 541810: Advertising agencies
- 541830: Media buying agencies
- 711510: Independent artists, writers, and performers

**Professional and Business Services:**
- 541600: Management and technical consulting
- 541211: Accounting and bookkeeping services
- 541110: Legal services

**Food and Hospitality:**
- 722511: Full-service restaurants
- 722513: Limited-service restaurants
- 721110: Hotels and motels

**Construction and Trades:**
- 238220: Plumbing and HVAC contractors
- 238210: Electrical contractors
- 236115: New single-family housing construction

---

## H2: How to Select Your Code

**Step 1:** Identify your primary revenue-generating activity.

**Step 2:** Browse the IRS Schedule C instructions or search the NAICS database at census.gov/naics.

**Step 3:** Choose the most specific code that describes your business. Avoid general codes if a specific one exists.

**Step 4:** Enter the 6-digit code on Schedule C, line B.

**Key Form:** Schedule C (Form 1040), Line B

---

## H2: Multiple Business Activities

If you operate multiple distinct businesses:

- **Related activities under one business:** Use the code for your primary activity
- **Separate unrelated businesses:** File a separate Schedule C for each business with its own code
- **Seasonal businesses:** Use the same code year-round even if your activities vary by season

**Example:** A freelance web designer who also sells handmade crafts online would file two Schedule Cs—one with code 541512 (computer systems design) and another with code 454110 (electronic shopping).

## H1

Does Florida Tax Social Security Benefits?

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**No, Florida does not tax Social Security benefits.** In fact, Florida has **no state income tax at all**, which means all retirement income—including Social Security, 401(k) withdrawals, pension payments, and IRA distributions—is completely free from state taxation. Only federal taxes may apply to your Social Security benefits depending on your total income.

---

## H2: Florida's No State Income Tax Policy

Florida is one of **nine states** with no state income tax:

**No State Income Tax States:**
- Alaska
- Florida
- Nevada
- South Dakota
- Tennessee
- Texas
- Washington
- Wyoming
- New Hampshire (only interest/dividends taxed)

**What This Means for Retirees:**
- **0%** state tax on Social Security benefits
- **0%** state tax on pension income
- **0%** state tax on 401(k) and IRA withdrawals
- **0%** state tax on investment income

---

## H2: Federal Taxation of Social Security in Florida

While Florida doesn't tax Social Security, federal taxes may apply:

**Federal Social Security Taxation Thresholds (2025):**

| Filing Status | 0% Taxed | Up to 50% Taxed | Up to 85% Taxed |
|---------------|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Single | Under $25,000 | $25,000 - $34,000 | Over $34,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Under $32,000 | $32,000 - $44,000 | Over $44,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $0 | - | Over $0* |

*Married filing separately who lived together at any time during the year

**Provisional Income Calculation:**
```
Provisional Income = Adjusted Gross Income
                   + Tax-exempt interest
                   + 50% of Social Security benefits
```

**Example:**
- Single retiree with $20,000 Social Security + $15,000 IRA withdrawal
- Provisional income: $15,000 + $10,000 (50% of SS) = $25,000
- Result: $0 of Social Security is federally taxable

## H2: Taxes Florida Retirees DO Pay

While Florida has no income tax, residents still pay:

**Sales Tax:**
- State rate: **6%**
- Local option: Up to **2%**
- Combined rate: **6% - 8%** depending on county

**Property Tax:**
- Average effective rate: **0.91%**
- **Homestead Exemption:** Up to $50,000 for primary residence
- **Save Our Homes:** 3% annual assessment increase cap

**Other Taxes:**
- Federal income tax (if applicable)
- Federal Social Security tax (if income exceeds thresholds)
- Gasoline tax (varies, approximately $0.36/gallon)

## H1

What Is NY PFL? New York Paid Family Leave Program Guide

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**NY PFL (New York Paid Family Leave)** is a state-mandated insurance program providing **12 weeks of paid leave** at **67% of your average weekly wage** for eligible employees. Funded entirely by a **0.373% payroll deduction** from employee wages (capped at the statewide average weekly wage), the program covers bonding with a new child, caring for a seriously ill family member, or handling needs related to a family member's military deployment. The 2025 maximum weekly benefit is **$1,177.05**.

---

## H2: What NY PFL Covers

**Eligible leave reasons:**

**1. Bonding with a new child:**
- Birth of a child (both parents eligible)
- Adoption or foster care placement
- Must be taken within first 12 months

**2. Caring for a family member:**
- Spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, parent-in-law, grandparent, or grandchild
- Must have a serious health condition requiring care
- Includes physical and psychological conditions

**3. Military deployment:**
- Qualifying exigency when family member is deployed abroad
- Covers activities related to deployment notification

---

## H2: 2025 NY PFL Benefits and Limits

**Benefit amount:** 67% of your average weekly wage (based on last 8 weeks worked)

**Maximum weekly benefit (2025):** $1,177.05

**Maximum duration:** 12 weeks per 52-week period

**Job protection:** Your employer must hold your job or provide equivalent position upon return

**Health insurance:** Continues during leave as if you were actively working

**Eligibility:** Full-time employees after 26 consecutive weeks; part-time employees after 175 days worked

---

## H2: NY PFL Payroll Deduction Rate

**Employee contribution rate (2025):** 0.373% of gross wages

**Wage cap:** Contributions apply only up to the New York State Average Weekly Wage (NYSAWW)
- 2025 cap: $1,757.19 per week
- Maximum annual employee contribution: ~$322

**Employer responsibility:**
- Collect employee contributions through payroll
- Remit to the insurance carrier
- May not deduct more than the statutory rate

**Key Form:** W-2 Box 14 (shows NYPFL contributions for informational purposes)

---

## H2: How to Apply for NY PFL

**Step 1:** Notify your employer at least 30 days before leave (if foreseeable) or as soon as possible

**Step 2:** Request the required forms from your employer or their insurance carrier

**Step 3:** Complete the Request for Paid Family Leave form

**Step 4:** Provide supporting documentation:
- Birth certificate or adoption papers (bonding leave)
- Certification of serious health condition (care leave)
- Military deployment documentation (military leave)

**Step 5:** Submit to the insurance carrier for approval

Benefits begin on the first day of qualifying leave (no waiting period).

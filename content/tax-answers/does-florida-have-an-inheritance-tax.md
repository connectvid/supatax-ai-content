## H1

Does Florida Have an Inheritance Tax?

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**No, Florida does not have an inheritance tax.** Florida also **does not have an estate tax**. This means beneficiaries who inherit assets in Florida pay **zero state taxes** on their inheritance, regardless of the amount inherited or their relationship to the deceased. Only federal estate tax may apply for estates exceeding $13.99 million in 2025.

---

## H2: What "No Inheritance Tax" Means for You

Florida's lack of inheritance tax provides significant benefits:

**For Heirs and Beneficiaries:**
- No state tax on cash inheritances
- No state tax on inherited real estate
- No state tax on inherited investments
- No state tax regardless of inheritance amount

**No Relationship Requirements:**
Unlike states with inheritance tax that tax distant relatives at higher rates:
- Spouses: 0% Florida tax
- Children: 0% Florida tax
- Siblings: 0% Florida tax
- Nieces/nephews: 0% Florida tax
- Friends/unrelated: 0% Florida tax

**Immediate Savings Example:**
If you inherit $500,000 in Pennsylvania as a sibling:
- **Pennsylvania:** $60,000 tax (12% sibling rate)
- **Florida:** $0 tax
- **Savings by inheriting in Florida:** $60,000

## H2: States That DO Have Inheritance Tax

Only six states impose inheritance tax as of 2025:

| State | Tax Rate | Exemptions |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Iowa | 0-15% | Spouses, lineal descendants exempt |
| Kentucky | 4-16% | Spouses, children/parents exempt up to $1,000 |
| Maryland | 0-10% | Spouses, close relatives exempt |
| Nebraska | 1-18% | Spouses exempt; others taxed |
| New Jersey | 11-16% | Spouses, children/parents exempt |
| Pennsylvania | 4.5-15% | Spouses exempt; lineal heirs at 4.5% |

**Pennsylvania Example (Closest Comparison):**
- Spouse inherits: 0% tax
- Child inherits: 4.5% tax
- Sibling inherits: 12% tax
- Other heir inherits: 15% tax

**Why Florida Is Different:**
Florida is one of 38 states with no inheritance tax, but it's unique in also having no state income tax and no estate tax.

## H2: Taxes That MAY Apply to Inherited Assets

While Florida doesn't tax inheritances directly, other taxes may apply:

**Federal Estate Tax:**
- Only for estates over $13.99 million (2025)
- Affects less than 0.1% of estates
- Paid by the estate, not the heir

**Capital Gains Tax (When Selling):**
- Applies when you sell inherited assets
- Based on stepped-up basis (value at date of death)
- Only appreciation AFTER inheritance is taxed

**Property Tax (Inherited Real Estate):**
- Annual property tax still applies
- Florida's average: 0.91% of assessed value
- Homestead exemption may apply for primary residence

**Income Tax (Inherited Retirement Accounts):**
- Traditional IRA/401(k) withdrawals are taxable income
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) apply
- Roth accounts may be tax-free

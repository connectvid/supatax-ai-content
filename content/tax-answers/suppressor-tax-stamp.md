## H1

Suppressor Tax Stamp: Cost, Process & How Long It Takes (2025)

---

## ANSWER SECTION

A **suppressor tax stamp** is a $200 federal tax payment required under the National Firearms Act (NFA) to legally purchase and possess a firearm suppressor (also called a silencer). The process involves submitting **ATF Form 4** to the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) along with fingerprints, passport photos, and the $200 tax payment. As of 2025, approval times range from **6 to 12 months**. Once approved, you receive a physical tax stamp that must remain with your suppressor as proof of legal registration.

---

## H2: How Much Does a Suppressor Tax Stamp Cost?

The **federal tax stamp costs $200** per suppressor. This is a one-time payment made to the ATF when you submit your application.

**Total Costs Breakdown:**
- ATF Tax Stamp: $200 (paid to federal government)
- ATF Form 4 submission: No additional fee
- Fingerprint cards (FD-258): $10-25 if done at local police station
- Passport photos: $10-15 at most pharmacies
- Gun trust (optional): $50-200 if using a trust instead of individual registration

**Important:** The $200 is a tax, not a fee. It goes to the U.S. Treasury, not the ATF. You cannot get this money back if your application is denied.

---

## H2: Step-by-Step Application Process

Follow these steps to obtain your suppressor tax stamp:

**Step 1: Purchase your suppressor from an FFL dealer**
You must buy from a Federal Firearms License holder. The suppressor stays at the dealer until your stamp is approved.

**Step 2: Complete ATF Form 4**
Fill out Form 4 (Application for Tax Paid Transfer and Registration of Firearm). You can file as an individual or through a gun trust.

**Step 3: Get fingerprints on FD-258 cards**
Visit your local police station or authorized fingerprinting location. You need two fingerprint cards.

**Step 4: Obtain passport photos**
Get two 2x2 inch passport-style photos taken within the last 6 months.

**Step 5: Submit to ATF with $200 payment**
Mail or eFile your complete application with:
- Completed Form 4
- Two FD-258 fingerprint cards
- Two passport photos
- $200 check or money order payable to "ATF"
- Copy of Form 4 sent to your local Chief Law Enforcement Officer (CLEO)

**Step 6: Wait for approval**
Processing times vary from 6-12 months for paper forms, 3-6 months for eFile.

**Step 7: Take possession**
Once approved, the dealer receives your stamp and transfers the suppressor to you.

---

## H2: Required Forms and Documentation

**ATF Form 4:** The primary application for transferring an NFA firearm to an individual or trust.

**FD-258 Fingerprint Cards:** Standard FBI fingerprint cards required for all NFA applications.

**CLEO Notification:** You must send a copy of your Form 4 to your local Chief Law Enforcement Officer for information purposes (no approval needed from CLEO since 41F rule changes).

**Responsible Person Questionnaire (RPQ):** Required for each responsible person on a gun trust, including additional fingerprint cards and photos for each person.

**NFA Gun Trust (optional):** A legal trust document that allows multiple people to possess and use the suppressor. Trusts require documentation and Responsible Person Questionnaires for all trustees.

---

## H2: Individual vs. Gun Trust Registration

| Feature | Individual | Gun Trust |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Application complexity** | Simpler | More complex |
| **Multiple users** | Only registered owner | Any trustee can possess |
| **Inheritance** | Requires estate transfer | Seamless transfer to beneficiaries |
| **Photos/fingerprints** | One set | Required for all responsible persons |
| **CLEO notification** | Required | Required for each responsible person |
| **Cost** | $200 stamp only | $200 stamp + trust creation ($50-200) |

**Recommendation:** Choose a gun trust if you want family members to legally possess or use the suppressor without you present, or for simplified estate planning.

---

## H2: State-Specific Rules

Suppressor ownership is **regulated at both federal and state levels**:

**States where suppressors are LEGAL (42 states):**
Most states allow suppressor ownership with the federal tax stamp, including Texas, Florida, Arizona, Pennsylvania, and Ohio.

**States where suppressors are ILLEGAL (8 states + DC):**
California, Delaware, Hawaii, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey, New York, Rhode Island, and Washington D.C. prohibit civilian suppressor possession entirely.

**States with additional restrictions:**
- **Vermont:** Legal for hunting but not for other purposes
- **Connecticut:** Legal but requires state permit in addition to federal stamp

**Important:** Even if you have a federal tax stamp, possessing a suppressor in a prohibited state is a felony.

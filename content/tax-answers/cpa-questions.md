## H1

Top CPA Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Accountant

---

## ANSWER SECTION

Before hiring a **CPA (Certified Public Accountant)**, ask about their **fee structure**, **experience with your specific tax situation**, and **service scope**. Key questions include: How do you charge (hourly vs. flat fee)? What are your credentials? Do you offer year-round tax planning? Will you represent me if I'm audited? The right CPA provides value beyond simple tax preparation through proactive advice and IRS representation rights.

---

## H2: Questions About Fees and Pricing

**1. What is your fee structure?**
- Hourly rates typically range $150-$400+ depending on location and experience
- Flat fees are common for standard returns: $200-$500 (individual), $500-$1,500 (Schedule C business)
- Ask what services are included and what triggers additional charges

**2. Are there additional fees for e-filing or state returns?**
Most CPAs include federal and one state in base pricing. Additional states usually cost $50-$100 each.

**3. Do you offer payment plans?**
Some CPAs allow payment over time, especially for complex returns requiring multiple sessions.

---

## H2: Questions About Credentials and Experience

**4. Are you a licensed CPA?**
Verify through your state board of accountancy. CPA designation requires education, exam passage, and ongoing continuing education.

**5. What experience do you have with my specific situation?**
Small business owners need different expertise than W-2 employees. Ask about their client base and specialization areas.

**6. Do you have a PTIN (Preparer Tax Identification Number)?**
All paid tax preparers must have a PTIN issued by the IRS.

**7. What is your continuing education focus?**
Tax laws change annually. Active CPAs complete 40 hours of CPE (Continuing Professional Education) yearly.

---

## H2: Questions About Services and Availability

**8. Do you offer year-round tax planning or just preparation?**
Proactive CPAs provide quarterly check-ins and strategic planning, not just annual filing.

**9. What is your availability during tax season?**
Ensure they can meet your deadlines. Some CPAs stop accepting new clients after March 1.

**10. Will you represent me if I'm audited?**
CPAs with unlimited representation rights can handle IRS audits. This is a key advantage over uncredentialed preparers.

**11. How do you communicate with clients?**
Email, phone, portal, or in-person? Clarify response time expectations.

---

## H2: Questions About Technology and Security

**12. What tax software do you use?**
Professional software (Drake, UltraTax, ProConnect) offers better diagnostics than consumer products.

**13. How do you protect my data?**
Look for encryption, secure portals, and written data security policies.

**14. Can I access my tax documents online?**
Secure client portals allow year-round access to prior year returns.

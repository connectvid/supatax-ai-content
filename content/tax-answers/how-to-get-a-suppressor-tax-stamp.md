## H1

How to Get a Suppressor Tax Stamp: Step-by-Step Guide

---

## ANSWER SECTION

To **get a suppressor tax stamp**, follow this 7-step process: (1) Purchase your suppressor from a licensed FFL dealer, (2) Complete **ATF Form 4**, (3) Get fingerprinted on **FD-258 cards**, (4) Obtain passport photos, (5) Submit your application with the **$200 tax payment**, (6) Wait **6 to 12 months** for ATF approval, and (7) Take possession from your dealer. You must be at least **21 years old**, legally allowed to possess firearms, and reside in one of the **42 states** where suppressor ownership is legal. The entire process is regulated under the National Firearms Act (NFA) of 1934.

---

## H2: Step 1: Purchase Your Suppressor

**Find an FFL dealer:** Locate a Federal Firearms License holder who sells NFA items. Most gun shops that sell suppressors are either Class 3 SOT dealers or have partnerships with Class 3 dealers.

**Select your suppressor:** Choose based on caliber compatibility, sound reduction, weight, and length. Popular brands include SilencerCo, Dead Air, SureFire, and Rugged Suppressors.

**Complete the purchase:** Pay for the suppressor, but it stays at the dealer. You'll complete paperwork to start the transfer process. The serial number is recorded and tied to your application.

**Timeline:** This step takes 1-2 days to select and purchase. The suppressor cannot leave the dealer until your stamp is approved.

---

## H2: Step 2: Complete ATF Form 4

**Download or access Form 4:** Available from the ATF website or through your dealer's eForms system.

**Fill out all required fields:**
- Section 1: Transferee (your) information—name, address, SSN
- Section 2: Transferor (dealer) information
- Section 3: Firearm description—manufacturer, model, serial number, caliber, barrel length, overall length
- Section 4: Reason for transfer (typically "for any lawful purpose")

**Choose your registration type:**
- Individual: Only you can possess the suppressor
- Gun Trust: Multiple trustees can possess it
- Corporation: Business entity owns the suppressor

**Sign and date:** You must sign under penalties of perjury that all information is true and correct.

**Timeline:** 30 minutes to 1 hour to complete accurately.

---

## H2: Step 3: Get Fingerprinted

**Where to get fingerprinted:**
- Local police/sheriff's department ($10-25)
- Some FFL dealers offer fingerprinting services
- Private fingerprinting businesses
- ATF-approved fingerprint channels

**Requirements:**
- Use FD-258 fingerprint cards (FBI standard)
- Get two completed cards
- Must be rolled fingerprints, not scanned
- Cards must be clean and legible

**For gun trusts:** Every responsible person (trustee) needs their own two fingerprint cards.

**Timeline:** 15-30 minutes at the fingerprinting location.

---

## H2: Step 4: Obtain Passport Photos

**Photo specifications:**
- 2x2 inches (standard passport size)
- Taken within the last 6 months
- Full face, front view, plain white background
- No glasses, hats, or head coverings (except religious)

**Where to get photos:**
- Pharmacies (CVS, Walgreens): $10-15
- Post office: $15
- Photography studios: $15-25
- Some FFL dealers offer photo services

**Quantity needed:** Two identical photos for individual applications; each responsible person on a trust needs two photos.

**Timeline:** 10-15 minutes.

---

## H2: Step 5: Submit Your Application

**For paper filing:**
Mail the following to: NFA Division, Bureau of ATF, P.O. Box 5015, Portland, OR 97208-5015
- Completed Form 4 (original)
- Two FD-258 fingerprint cards
- Two passport photos
- $200 check or money order payable to "ATF"

**For eForms filing:**
- Register at eforms.atf.gov
- Upload scanned documents
- Pay $200 via credit card
- Print and mail fingerprint cards to ATF within 10 days

**CLEO notification:**
Send a copy of your Form 4 to your local Chief Law Enforcement Officer (sheriff or police chief) for informational purposes only. They do not approve or deny your application.

**Timeline:** Same day for eForms; 3-5 days mailing for paper.

---

## H2: Step 6: Wait for ATF Approval

**Current wait times (2025):**
- eForms: 3-6 months average
- Paper forms: 6-12 months average

**What happens during review:**
- Background check through NICS
- Verify all documents are complete
- Check for disqualifying factors
- Process payment

**Check your status:**
- eForms: Log in to your account for real-time status
- Paper: Call ATF NFA Branch at (304) 616-4500
- Have your serial number and name ready

**What to do while waiting:**
- Research suppressor maintenance
- Prepare your range equipment
- Do NOT take possession until approved

---

## H2: Step 7: Take Possession

**Notification:** Your dealer will call when the approved Form 4 arrives with the tax stamp attached.

**Complete Form 4473:** Fill out the standard firearm transfer form at the dealer.

**Present identification:** Show valid government-issued photo ID.

**Receive your suppressor and stamp:**
- Physical suppressor
- Approved Form 4 with tax stamp (keep this safe)
- Make copies for your records

**Important reminders:**
- Store the original stamp in a fireproof location
- The stamp must remain with the suppressor
- You're now legally registered and can use your suppressor

**Timeline:** 30 minutes at the dealer.

## H1

Is Private School Tuition Tax Deductible?

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**Private school tuition is NOT tax deductible** on your federal income tax return. Personal education expenses, including private school tuition for K-12, are considered **personal expenses** and are not eligible for a federal tax deduction. However, there are **two workarounds**: First, you can use **529 plan funds** to pay for up to **$10,000 per year** in K-12 private school tuition per student. Second, some **states offer tax credits or deductions** for private school tuition or contributions to scholarship-granting organizations.

---

## H2: Federal Tax Treatment

**Not Deductible:**
- Private school tuition (K-12)
- Private school fees and books
- Homeschooling expenses
- After-school programs
- Tutoring costs

**Why Not Deductible:**
- Personal education expenses are not business expenses
- No federal credit or deduction for K-12 education
- TCJA eliminated potential workarounds through 2025

**Exception - Special Needs:**
- Tuition for special schools may be deductible as medical expense
- Must be prescribed by a doctor
- Must be primarily for medical care, not education
- Subject to 7.5% AGI floor

---

## H2: Using 529 Plans for K-12 Private School

**529 Plan K-12 Provision:**
- Up to **$10,000 per year** per student
- For K-12 private school tuition only
- Does not cover books, supplies, or fees

**How It Works:**
- Contribute to 529 plan (state tax deduction may apply in some states)
- Funds grow tax-free
- Withdraw up to $10,000/year for K-12 tuition
- No federal tax on earnings used for qualified expenses

**Example:**
- 529 plan balance: $50,000 (contributions + growth)
- Annual K-12 tuition: $15,000
- Withdraw $10,000 from 529 plan (tax-free)
- Pay remaining $5,000 from other funds

**Important:**
- Only tuition qualifies (not room/board for K-12)
- $10,000 limit is per student, not per account
- States may have different rules for state tax purposes

---

## H2: State Tax Credits and Deductions

Some states offer tax benefits for private school:

**States with Tax Credits for Scholarship Donations:**
- Arizona, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Louisiana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Virginia

**States with Education Tax Credits/Deductions:**
- Illinois: Education Expense Credit up to $750
- Indiana: School Scholarship Tax Credit
- Louisiana: School Tuition Deduction
- Minnesota: Education Credit (K-12)
- Wisconsin: Private School Tuition Deduction

**How Scholarship Tax Credits Work:**
1. Donate to approved scholarship organization
2. Receive state tax credit (often 50-100% of donation)
3. Scholarship organization provides scholarships to students
4. You don't need a child in school to benefit

---

## H2: Coverdell Education Savings Accounts

**Coverdell ESAs** offer another option:

**Features:**
- Contribute up to $2,000 per year per beneficiary
- Contributions not tax-deductible
- Earnings grow tax-free
- Withdrawals tax-free for K-12 and higher education expenses

**K-12 Qualified Expenses:**
- Tuition and fees
- Books and supplies
- Tutoring
- Computer equipment
- Internet access

**Income Limits (2025):**
- Phase-out begins at $95,000 single / $190,000 married
- Full phase-out at $110,000 single / $220,000 married

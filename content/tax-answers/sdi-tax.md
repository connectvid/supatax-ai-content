SDI Tax: What It Is and How It Works in California (2025)

---

## ANSWER SECTION

**SDI tax** (State Disability Insurance) is a mandatory California payroll tax that provides short-term disability and paid family leave benefits to workers. For 2025, California employees pay **1.1% of wages up to $153,164**, making the maximum annual contribution **$1,684.80**. Your employer withholds this from each paycheck. If you become disabled or need family leave, you can claim benefits that replace 60-70% of your wages.

---

## H2: What Does SDI Tax Cover?

California SDI provides wage replacement through two programs:

**State Disability Insurance (DI):**
- Replaces income when you cannot work due to illness, injury, or pregnancy
- Covers non-work-related conditions only (work injuries fall under workers' compensation)
- Pays 60-70% of your base wages
- Maximum duration: 52 weeks
- 7-day waiting period before benefits start

**Paid Family Leave (PFL):**
- Replaces income when caring for a seriously ill family member
- Replaces income for bonding with a new child (birth, adoption, foster care)
- Same 60-70% wage replacement as DI
- Maximum duration: 8 weeks per year

Both programs are funded by your SDI payroll deductions — there are no additional costs when you file a claim.

---

## H2: 2025 SDI Tax Rates and Limits

**Employee contribution rate:** 1.1% of wages
**Maximum taxable wages:** $153,164
**Maximum annual employee contribution:** $1,684.80

**2025 SDI Rate History:**
- 2021: 1.2%
- 2022: 1.1%
- 2023: 0.9%
- 2024: 1.1%
- 2025: 1.1%

The rate fluctuates based on the health of the SDI fund and projected benefit claims.

**Example calculations for 2025:**
- $50,000 salary: $50,000 × 1.1% = $550 annual SDI tax
- $100,000 salary: $100,000 × 1.1% = $1,100 annual SDI tax
- $153,164+ salary: $153,164 × 1.1% = $1,684.80 maximum

## H2: How to File an SDI Claim

If you need disability or family leave benefits:

**Step 1: File online**
Visit SDI Online at edd.ca.gov and create an account

**Step 2: Submit Part A (Claimant Statement)**
Provide personal information and details about your disability or leave

**Step 3: Have your doctor submit Part B (Medical Certificate)**
For disability claims, your doctor must certify your condition online or by mail

**Step 4: Serve the 7-day waiting period**
No benefits paid for the first 7 days of disability

**Step 5: Receive payment**
Benefits arrive via EDD Debit Card or direct deposit, typically within 14 days of claim approval

**Important deadlines:**
- File within 49 days of disability/start of leave
- Late claims may be denied

## H2: SDI vs. Other California Payroll Deductions

California employees see multiple deductions on their paystubs:

| Deduction | Rate (2025) | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Federal Income Tax | Varies | Federal tax withholding |
| Social Security | 6.2% (up to $176,100) | Retirement benefits |
| Medicare | 1.45% (no cap) | Health insurance for 65+ |
| CA State Income Tax | 1-13.3% | California taxes |
| CA SDI | 1.1% (up to $153,164) | Disability + family leave |
| CASDI | Same as SDI | Alternative abbreviation |

Note: Some paystubs show "CASDI" — this is the same as SDI (California State Disability Insurance).

## SECTION: Get Instant Answers

**H2:** Popular Tax Questions Answered

Showcase 6 high-traffic Q&A pages with direct answers:

1. **What is backup withholding?**
   Backup withholding is a 24% federal tax the IRS requires payers to withhold when you haven't provided a valid TIN.
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/what-is-backup-withholding/)

2. **Is homeowners insurance tax deductible?**
   Homeowners insurance is NOT deductible for your personal residence. Rental property insurance IS deductible on Schedule E.
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/is-homeowners-insurance-tax-deductible/)

3. **How long does it take for tax refund to show in bank account?**
   After IRS approval, refunds typically appear in 1-5 business days. E-filed returns: 21 days total.
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/how-long-tax-refund-show-bank-account/)

4. **What is Medicare tax?**
   Medicare tax is 1.45% on all wages. High earners pay an additional 0.9%. Self-employed pay 2.9% total.
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/what-is-medicare-tax/)

5. **Are funeral expenses tax deductible?**
   Funeral expenses are NOT deductible on personal returns. They ARE deductible on estate tax returns (Form 706).
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/are-funeral-expenses-tax-deductible/)

6. **If I pay child support, can I claim my child on taxes?**
   Paying child support does NOT automatically give you the right to claim the child. The custodial parent has that right by default.
   [Read full answer](/tax-answers/if-i-pay-child-support-can-i-claim-my-child-on-taxes/)
